Total Knee Replacement

  1. Total Knee replacement, or knee arthroplasty, is a surgical process to replace the weight-bearing surfaces of the knee joint to relieve pain and disability. It is most generally executed for osteoarthritis, and also for other knee diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis and psoriatic arthritis. In patients with severe deformation from advanced rheumatoid arthritis, trauma, or long-standing osteoarthritis, this surgery may be more complicated and carry a higher risk. Osteoporosis does not generally cause knee pain, deformity, or inflammation and is not a basis to perform knee replacement.

Total Hip Replacement

Total Hip Replacement (THR) is a surgical procedure in which a damaged or worn-out hip joint is replaced with an artificial joint, also known as a prosthesis. This procedure is commonly performed in patients suffering from severe arthritis, hip fractures, or other joint conditions that cause chronic pain and limit mobility. During the surgery, both the ball and socket of the hip joint are replaced with metal, ceramic, or plastic components designed to mimic the natural movement of the hip. Total hip replacement helps relieve pain, restore joint function, and improve overall quality of life. With proper care and rehabilitation, most patients regain their mobility and return to normal daily activities within a few months.
