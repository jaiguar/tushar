Basics Of Rehabilitation Therapy

Occupational Therapy

Helps patients return to daily tasks and work-related functions after orthopedic injuries to the hand, wrist, shoulder, or spine.

  • Daily Function
  • Upper Limb Rehab
  • Independence Support

Orthopedic Rehabilitation

Focused therapy post-surgery (e.g., knee, hip, or shoulder replacement) to promote healing and improve joint function.

  • Joint Recovery
  • Post-Surgery Care
  • Mobility Restoration

Geriatric Orthopedic Rehab

Specialized rehabilitation for elderly patients recovering from hip fractures, osteoporosis-related injuries, or joint replacements.

  • Fall Prevention
  • Strength Building
  • Mobility Enhancement

    RESEARCH

    Comparative Study Using Intramedullary K-wire Fixation Over Titanium Elastic Nail in Paediatric Shaft Femur Fractures, JCDR

    01

    Objective Definition

    To compare the clinical and radiological outcomes of intramedullary K-wire fixation versus Titanium Elastic Nailing (TEN) in treating pediatric shaft femur fractures.

    02

    Study Design & Methodology

    A prospective study conducted on pediatric patients with femoral shaft fractures. Patients were grouped based on treatment received: K-wire fixation vs. Titanium Elastic Nail.

    03

    Data Analysis & Results

    Results were compared in terms of fracture union time, post-operative complications, cost-effectiveness, and functional recovery. Statistical tools were used to evaluate significance.

    04

    Conclusion & Implication

    The study concluded which method offers better clinical outcomes in pediatric femoral fractures—helping guide orthopedic surgeons in choosing an appropriate fixation method.
